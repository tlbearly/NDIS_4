Land and Access Layers

CPW Public Access Properties: The purpose of this CPW dataset is to show Colorado Parks and Wildlife property boundaries managed for use by the public. This dataset is created from property deeds and should not be considered survey quality data. See update schedule below.

Land Management (COMAP): The Colorado Natural Heritage Program (CNHP) and the Geospatial Centroid at Colorado State University (CSU) are the current stewards of COMaP. COMaP is the state’s premier map of protected lands, featuring over 28,000 entries of protected lands parcels from over 300 different data sources. Data was updated last on 07/02/2024.

Township Range Sections: In the Public Land Survey System (PLSS) of the United States, a township, range, and section are units of land measurement. A Township is a horizontal row of sections in the PLSS and a Range is a vertical column. Each Township and Range consists of a 6-mile by 6-mile square of land. A Section is a square tract of land that is approximately one mile by one mile and contains 640 acres. Sections are numbered consecutively from 1 to 36, starting in the northeast corner

Land and Access Layers

CPW Public Access Properties: The purpose of this CPW dataset is to show Colorado Parks and Wildlife property boundaries managed for use by the public. This dataset is created from property deeds and should not be considered survey quality data.

GMU boundary: Colorado is divided into hunting areas called Game Management Units (GMU). Some licenses are valid in one unit only, while others are valid in multiple units. This CPW dataset tracks and maps the most up-to-date GMU bounaries as defined in Chapter W-00 - General Provisions. Refer to the Colorado Big Game Hunting Brochure for details: https://cpw.state.co.us/hunting/big-game

Land Management (COMAP): The Colorado Natural Heritage Program (CNHP) and the Geospatial Centroid at Colorado State University (CSU) are the current stewards of COMaP. COMaP is the state’s premier map of protected lands, featuring over 28,000 entries of protected lands parcels from over 300 different data sources.

CPW Walk In Access: The purpose of this CPW datset is to provide boundaries for private land enrolled in the small game Walk-in Access program (WIA). The fields depicted are intended for general reference and do not necessarily depict property boundaries. Field conditions can change often. Hunters should pay attention to the signs posted in the field. They are the deciding factor that determines access as enrolled properties. If a field is posted with a closed sign, there is no hunting allowed in that field, despite what the data depicts.

Township Range Sections: In the Public Land Survey System (PLSS) of the United States, a township, range, and section are units of land measurement. A Township is a horizontal row of sections in the PLSS and a Range is a vertical column. Each Township and Range consists of a 6-mile by 6-mile square of land. A Section is a square tract of land that is approximately one mile by one mile and contains 640 acres. Sections are numbered consecutively from 1 to 36, starting in the northeast corner

Colorado Parks and Wildlife Public GIS Data Update Schedule
**CPW maps and GIS data: https://cpw.state.co.us/maps-and-gis**

Administrative Boundary GIS Data

  1. First Week in February – to match any changes in Sheep, Goat, or Big Game GMU boundaries published in the Big Game Brochure.
  2. First Week in March – to update changes in administrative boundaries Regions, Areas, and Districts.
  3. First Week in August – to update Public Access Properties at the beginning of Big Game hunting seasons.
  4. First Week in September – to update Walk In Access program property boundaries.
  5. First Week in November – to update Walk In Access program property boundaries (late season).

Wildlife Species GIS Data

  1. Middle of December – at the completion of regional species activity mapping updates
